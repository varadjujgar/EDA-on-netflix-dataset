# EDA-on-netflix-dataset

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis on Netflix Dataset

A comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the Netflix titles dataset (November 2019), uncovering trends, patterns, and insights about Netflix's content library.

---

## 📁 Dataset

- **File**: `netflix_titles_nov_2019.csv`
- **Source**: Netflix titles dataset (as of November 2019)
- **Contents**: Information about movies and TV shows available on Netflix including title, director, cast, country, release year, rating, duration, and genre.

---

## 🔍 Analysis Overview

The notebook `Netflix.ipynb` covers the following:

- **Data Cleaning** – Handling missing values, duplicates, and formatting issues
- **Univariate Analysis** – Distribution of content types, ratings, genres, and release years
- **Bivariate Analysis** – Relationships between variables (e.g., content type vs. country)
- **Temporal Trends** – How Netflix's content library evolved over the years
- **Geographic Insights** – Top content-producing countries
- **Genre Analysis** – Most popular genres and categories
- **Visualizations** – Charts and graphs to illustrate key findings

---

## 📌 Key Findings

- Netflix has significantly more **Movies** than **TV Shows** in its library.
- The **United States** is the top content-producing country on Netflix.
- Content production saw a massive surge from **2015 onwards**.
- The most common rating is **TV-MA**, targeting mature audiences.
- **Drama** and **Comedy** are the most prevalent genres.
